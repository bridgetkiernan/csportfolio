Horses


The horse is one of two extant subspecies of Equus ferus.
It is an odd-toed ungulate mammal belonging to the taxonomic
family Equidae. The horse has evolved over the past 45 to 55
million years from a small multi-toed creature, Eohippus,
into the large, single-toed animal of today. They are gourgous
animals that have been domesticated for around 5,000 years.

Fun Facts About Horses

  • Horses can sleep both lying down and standing up.
  • Horses can run shortly after birth.
  • Domestic horses have a lifespan of around 25 years.

Pasta


Pasta is a staple food of traditional Italian cuisine, with
the first reference dating to 1154 in Sicily. There are many
different types of pastas and sauces. It is by far the best
food in the world!

Fun Facts About Pasta

  • Americans consume 6 billion pounds of pasta each year.
  • According to the International Pasta Organization,
    there are more more than 600 different shapes of pasta
    produced throughout the world.
